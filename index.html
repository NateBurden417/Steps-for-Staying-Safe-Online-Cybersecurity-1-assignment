Steps for Staying Safe Online


Some of this information may not be up to date based on the time you are looking at it.

Author: 5xdf

Publisher: 5xdf

Published on: September 19th 2023




There are multiple ways of staying online, here are 5 of the best ways to stay safe online.


1. Personally Identifiable Information (PII)


Personally Identifiable Information is any information that identifies a specific individual.


This can range from anything like your Student ID to your SSN (Social Security Number)


Not securing this information can lead to identity fraud, bank funds being stolen, and more.


Personally Identifiable Information should only be shared with trusted, and verified individuals


If someone requires your Credit Card number, SSN, or any other sensitive details about you, you should first, check if the source is verified. If it is a phone number and it says "Number Verified" search up the number online, if you get no negative results you are most likely fine.


2. Password Saving


The Google password manager isn't secure as it doesn't require any verification before it autofills your username and password. Because it doesn't use any verification methods, information can be easily stolen by anyone who has your password.


If you do want to save your password use a trusted password manager (like ExpressVPN - requires a biometric for your passwords).


3. Risky Sites


Always check to make sure that the website you are on either shows a lock in front of the website or has https:// instead of http://


http:// stands for Hypertext Transfer Protocall, while https:// stands for the same as http:// but secure.


Alternatively, if you see an IP address (looks like 123.123.123.123 usually) then leave the site instantly as the person who is running the site may have a keylogger plugin on the website.


Keyloggers are programs that track every single key you press and show them to the user who is controlling the website.


4. Free Wi-Fi Spoofing


If you are using free Wi-Fi always check the URL at the top of your screen on the network sign-in. If there is an IP leave the site quickly. If you see http://sit3nam3.com also leave the site quickly.


Also, if you are using a free Wi-Fi always use a VPN so if your information does get sent to anyone, the information will be obfuscated and be unreadable by the creator of the spoofed Wi-Fi.


5. Social Media


Social media is a place for users to connect.


Only post photos of trips to family or post them to the public after you are back home.


Make sure you choose a strong password when making a social media account, as social media accounts are usually the main culprit of being impersonated/hacked.
